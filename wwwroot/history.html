Welcome to Sarawak!



SARAWAK is one of the 13 states of Malaysia, located on the northwest coast of Borneo, the third-largest island in the world. It is the largest state in Malaysia by land area, known for its rich biodiversity, dense rainforests, and diverse cultures. Sarawak is home to many indigenous groups such as the Iban, Bidayuh, Orang Ulu, and Melanau, as well as Malays and Chinese communities.

Sarawak, located on the island of Borneo, has a long history dating back over 40,000 years, with early human settlement evident in sites like the Niah Caves. It was part of the Brunei Sultanate from the 15th century until 1841, when British adventurer James Brooke became the first White Rajah and established the Brooke Dynasty, ruling Sarawak as a personal kingdom for over a century. During World War II, Sarawak was occupied by Japan (1941â€“1945) before becoming a British Crown Colony in 1946, a move that triggered local resistance. On 16 September 1963, Sarawak gained independence from British rule and became one of the founding states of Malaysia.

Today, Sarawak is an economic hub with industries like oil & gas, timber, palm oil, and tourism. It has maintained autonomy over immigration laws and land rights, distinguishing it from Peninsular Malaysia. The state capital, Kuching, is a cultural and historical center with museums, colonial architecture, and vibrant markets.
