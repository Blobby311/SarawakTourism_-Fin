Festivals in Sarawak

Sarawak is a land of rich traditions and diverse cultures, where vibrant festivals bring communities together in celebration. These events showcase the unique heritage, music, dance, and rituals of the region, offering visitors an immersive cultural experience. From lively gatherings to deep-rooted traditions, Sarawak's festivals reflect the spirit and unity of its people.



GAWAI DAYAK FESTIVAL



Gawai Dayak is a harvest festival celebrated by the Dayak people of Sarawak, Malaysia, and West Kalimantan, Indonesia. It is held annually on June 1st and 2nd to mark the end of the rice harvesting season and to give thanks for a bountiful harvest while praying for future prosperity.

The festival includes traditional rituals, ngajat (a warrior dance), music, feasting, and drinking tuak (rice wine). Families gather to honor ancestors, perform blessings, and partake in communal festivities.

Initially rejected as a public holiday during colonial rule, it was officially recognized in 1965. Today, Gawai Dayak remains a vibrant celebration of indigenous identity and heritage.



PESTA KAUL



Pesta Kaul or Kaul Festival is a traditional Melanau festival celebrated in Sarawak, Malaysia, marking the end of the monsoon season and seeking blessings for the year ahead. It is typically held in April and involves offerings placed on a seraheng (decorated ceremonial raft) to appease spirits and ensure prosperity.

The festival features traditional rituals, music, dance, games, and feasting, with highlights like the Tibou, a giant swing symbolizing unity and strength. Once a solemn religious ritual, Kaul has evolved into a cultural event showcasing Melanau heritage while preserving its spiritual significance.



RAINFOREST WORLD MUSIC FESTIVAL



The Rainforest World Music Festival (RWMF) is an annual three-day music festival held in Sarawak Cultural Village, Malaysia.

Established in 1998, it celebrates world music, featuring a mix of traditional and contemporary sounds from global and indigenous musicians. The festival includes live performances, cultural workshops, dance, and interactive sessions, set against the backdrop of the Borneo rainforest. It attracts thousands of music enthusiasts and promotes cultural diversity, heritage preservation, and eco-tourism.

RWMF is one of Asia’s most renowned music festivals, blending nature, music, and indigenous traditions.



SARAWAK REGATTA



The Sarawak Regatta is an annual boat race held on the Sarawak River in Kuching, Malaysia.

Dating back to the 19th century, it was introduced by the Brooke administration to promote peace among warring tribes through friendly competition. The event features traditional longboat races, with teams paddling in unison to win the coveted "Raja Sungai" (King of the River) title.

It also includes cultural performances, exhibitions, and water sports, attracting both locals and tourists. The regatta remains a symbol of unity, history, and Sarawak’s rich maritime heritage.



BORNEO JAZZ FESTIVAL



The Borneo Jazz Festival is an annual music event held in Miri, Sarawak, Malaysia, celebrating jazz music with international and local artists.

Established in 2006, it aims to promote jazz culture while boosting tourism in Borneo. The festival features diverse jazz styles, from traditional to contemporary fusion, along with music workshops, jam sessions, and cultural exhibitions.

Set against a scenic coastal backdrop, it attracts music lovers worldwide, making it one of the premier jazz festivals in Southeast Asia.
